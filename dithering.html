Dithering

Dithering was originally developed for the purpose of displaying images on screens with limited color depth (for example: Coloured -> Black & White).

But now it is also used as an artistic effect, giving images a retro, pixelated look.

Implementation

A simple way to implement Black & White dithering is to follow these steps:

  1. Convert the image to grayscale

    Let's consider a sample $8 \times 8$ grayscale image $I$:

    $$ I = \begin{pmatrix} 0.1 & 0.1 & 0.2 & 0.2 & 0.7 & 0.7 & 0.8 & 0.8 \\ 0.1 & 0.1 & 0.2 & 0.2 & 0.7 & 0.7 & 0.8 & 0.8 \\ 0.3 & 0.3 & 0.4 & 0.4 & 0.9 & 0.9 & 1.0 & 1.0 \\ 0.3 & 0.3 & 0.4 & 0.4 & 0.9 & 0.9 & 1.0 & 1.0 \\ 0.1 & 0.1 & 0.2 & 0.2 & 0.7 & 0.7 & 0.8 & 0.8 \\ 0.1 & 0.1 & 0.2 & 0.2 & 0.7 & 0.7 & 0.8 & 0.8 \\ 0.3 & 0.3 & 0.4 & 0.4 & 0.9 & 0.9 & 1.0 & 1.0 \\ 0.3 & 0.3 & 0.4 & 0.4 & 0.9 & 0.9 & 1.0 & 1.0 \end{pmatrix} $$

    $$ M = \frac{1}{16} \times \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 8 & 2 & 10 \\ 12 & 4 & 14 & 6 \\ 3 & 11 & 1 & 9 \\ 15 & 7 & 13 & 5 \end{pmatrix} $$
